Your Easy Guide to SARS eFiling

SARS eFiling is the electronic platform for submitting your tax returns online. It's secure, convenient, and faster than manual submissions. Follow this step-by-step guide to complete your 2025 tax return.

Step 1: Register for eFiling

First-time users:

  1. Visit www.sars.gov.za and click on "eFiling"
  2. Click "Register" and select "Individual"
  3. Enter your ID number, income tax reference number, and contact details
  4. Create a username and password
  5. SARS will send you a PIN via SMS or email
  6. Use the PIN to activate your eFiling profile

What you'll need:

  • South African ID number or passport
  • Income tax reference number (if you have one)
  • Valid email address
  • South African cellphone number
  • Banking details

Step 2: Gather Your Documents

Essential documents for 2025 tax year:

  • IRP5 Certificate: From your employer (shows salary, tax deducted, etc.)
  • IT3(a) Certificates: For interest earned from banks
  • IT3(b) Certificates: For dividends received
  • Medical Aid Certificate: Shows contributions and benefits
  • Retirement Fund Certificate: Shows pension/provident fund contributions
  • RA Fund Certificate: For retirement annuity contributions
  • Rental Income Records: Income and expense statements
  • Investment Statements: Capital gains/losses, foreign investments
  • Business Records: If you're self-employed or have business income
  • Donation Receipts: Section 18A receipts for tax-deductible donations

Step 3: Log In and Start Your Return

  1. Go to www.sars.gov.za and click "eFiling"
  2. Enter your username and password
  3. Click on "Returns" in the main menu
  4. Select "Income Tax Return (ITR12)" for individuals
  5. Choose the 2025 tax year
  6. Click "Create Return" to start
Tip: SARS may pre-populate some information from third parties (employers, banks, etc.). Always verify this information is correct and complete.

Step 4: Complete Each Section

Main sections to complete:

Personal Information
  • Verify your personal details
  • Update contact information if needed
  • Confirm banking details for refunds
Income (Revenue)
  • Employment income from IRP5
  • Interest and dividends
  • Rental income
  • Business income
  • Foreign income
Deductions
  • Retirement fund contributions
  • Medical aid contributions
  • Donations to PBOs
  • Business expenses
  • Home office expenses
Tax Credits
  • Medical scheme fees tax credit
  • Additional medical expenses
  • Foreign tax credits

Step 5: Review and Submit

  1. Review all sections: Check every entry for accuracy
  2. Use the calculator: eFiling will calculate your tax automatically
  3. Check the summary: Review your total tax liability or refund
  4. Validate the return: eFiling will check for errors
  5. Submit your return: Click "Submit" when you're satisfied
  6. Print/save confirmation: Keep the acknowledgment of receipt
Important: Once submitted, you cannot make changes easily. You may need to request a revision or file an objection for significant changes.

Step 6: After Submission

What happens next:

  • Assessment: SARS will process your return (usually within 21 business days)
  • Notification: You'll receive an SMS/email when your assessment is ready
  • Check your assessment: Log into eFiling to view your Notice of Assessment
  • Refunds: If you're due a refund, it will be paid into your registered bank account
  • Payments: If you owe tax, pay by the due date to avoid penalties and interest

Payment options if you owe tax:

  • eFiling online payment
  • Internet banking
  • ATM payments
  • Bank branches
  • SARS branches

Important Tips for Success

Keep Records

Maintain all supporting documents for 5 years. SARS may request them during audits or reviews.

File Early

Submit your return well before the deadline to avoid penalties and receive refunds sooner.

Double-Check

Verify all amounts against your certificates and statements. Errors can trigger audits or penalties.

Save Progress

eFiling automatically saves your work, but manually save regularly to avoid losing data.

Get Help

Use SARS help resources, tax practitioners, or the Contact Centre (0800 00 7277) if you need assistance.

Update Details

Keep your contact details and banking information current to ensure smooth communication and refund payments.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Incorrect banking details: Double-check account numbers for refund payments
  • Missing income: Include ALL income sources, even small amounts
  • Claiming invalid deductions: Only claim legitimate, supported expenses
  • Ignoring pre-populated data: Verify and correct any incorrect pre-populated information
  • Late submission: File before the deadline to avoid penalties
  • Not keeping records: Maintain supporting documents for all claims
